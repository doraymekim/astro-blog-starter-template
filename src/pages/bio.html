Composer Biography

Ray D. Kim a composer with an amalgamation of styles & colors.

Ray is a film composer, characterized by a distinct hybrid voice, blending traditional and contemporary elements into a richly melodic amalgamation of styles. His portfolio includes notable productions like AppleTV's "Lessons in Chemistry," HBO Max's "Hacks" Seasons 1-5, and Netflix's "Griselda," demonstrating his remarkable versatility across various styles and genres.

In addition to his composition work, Ray is a skilled orchestrator who has contributed to projects such as Universal's “Speak No Evil,” Netflix’s “Terminator Zero,” and AppleTV’s “Presumed Innocent” series.

Currently, Ray teaches at the University of Miami, Frost School of Music as an instructor for Orchestration & Video Game Scoring and is actively involved in composing for the season of Netflix's “Department Q” and orchestrating BBC & AppleTV’s “Dolphin” series. With a promising lineup of film projects on the horizon, he is also working on a video game “Prometheus” set to release on Xbox, Playstation Steam and Nintendo.

Professional profile

Ray D. Kim develops cinematic scores that fuse orchestral grandeur with modern sound design. His work is defined by memorable themes, dynamic textures, and an emotional arc that supports every visual moment with precision.

He crafts music for streaming television and feature projects with the same attention to detail he brings to orchestration, creating music that feels cinematic, purposeful, and instantly recognizable.

AppleTV's "Lessons in Chemistry," HBO Max's "Hacks" Seasons 1-3, Netflix's "Griselda".

Universal's “Speak No Evil,” Netflix’s “Terminator Zero,” AppleTV’s “Presumed Innocent”.

Signature Bio

Ray bridges film, television, and interactive music with training from Indiana University’s Jacobs School of Music and the University of Miami’s Frost School of Music.

He teaches film scoring and video game scoring at the University of Miami, while maintaining membership in the Emmy TV Academy and the Grammy Recording Academy.

Current Focus

Ray is currently driving the sound of Netflix’s Fight for 84 and New Line Cinema’s The Revenge of La Llorona, while composing for Bleeding Fingers and crafting the immersive score for the game Prometheus.

His current work blends studio storytelling with interactive worlds, balancing cinematic detail and forward-looking sonic design.

Recent Impact

Recent highlights include Hacks S1-5, Griselda, Department Q S1, Monsieur Spade, and Lessons in Chemistry, where he contributed both additional music and orchestration to major streaming and studio projects.

Those collaborations span Netflix, Warner Bros., HBO Max, AppleTV, Amazon Prime, Universal, A24, Disney, and Hulu.
